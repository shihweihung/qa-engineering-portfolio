# Case Studies

Generalized analyses of real bugs, incidents, and debugging work.

Each case study describes a class of problem — not a specific employer, product, or team. The goal is to capture the engineering reasoning: how the bug was found, why it existed, and what changed as a result.

## What makes a good case study

- A clear problem statement that someone outside the team can follow
- The detection method: how was it caught, and what would have caught it earlier
- Root cause analysis: what actually went wrong
- The fix and the lesson: what changed, and what would be done differently
